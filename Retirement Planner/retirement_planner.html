Caveats. Real returns of 7% are the long-run average for stocks. A balanced portfolio with bonds runs closer to 5%. The first decade after you retire matters most: a bad sequence of returns can wreck a plan that looks healthy on paper. Income tax in retirement is not modelled and depends on whether the money sits in a TFSA, RRSP, or non-registered account.

Method. Income trajectory is linear from current to peak, then flat at peak, then a step down. Tax uses Ontario combined marginal brackets (an approximation, editable in source). Saving is a fixed share of after-tax income. CPP scales with contribution years (39 years at the max earnings ceiling gets you the full benefit, fewer years prorate it). CPP and OAS are then valued as deferred life annuities and pooled with the portfolio to find a level lifetime real income. OAS clawback is an estimate above the threshold.
