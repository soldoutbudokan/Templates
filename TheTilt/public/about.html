About TILT

win probability added for IPL cricket

what is TILT?

TILT measures how much a player tilts the game in their team's favour. For every ball bowled in the IPL, we calculate the change in win probability and attribute it to the batter and bowler involved.

A player's TILT score is their average total win probability added per match. A TILT of +5.00% means the player adds 5 percentage points of win probability to their team every match they play.

how it works

For each ball:
delta = win_prob_after - win_prob_before

Batter credit: +delta (positive = helped batting team)
Bowler credit: -delta (positive = helped bowling team)

The win probability model is a LightGBM classifier trained on all historical IPL ball-by-ball data. Features include: balls remaining, wickets in hand, current run rate, required run rate, per-over effects, venue, and recent form.

ranking methodology

Bayesian shrinkage: Raw TILT estimates are shrunk toward the population mean using empirical Bayes (James-Stein). This stabilizes rankings for players with fewer matches.

90% confidence interval: The default ranking uses the lower bound of each player's 90% confidence interval. This naturally penalizes small samples — a player needs consistent performance over many matches to rank highly.

Minimum thresholds: Players need 10+ matches to appear in rankings. Per-role stats (batting/bowling TILT) require 50+ balls in that role.

interpreting the numbers

Batting TILT: How much a batter shifts win probability when at the crease. Big positive = consistently scores runs when it matters.

Bowling TILT: How much a bowler shifts win probability in their team's favour. Big positive = takes wickets and contains runs at key moments.

Total TILT: Combined batting + bowling impact per match. The best all-rounders dominate this ranking.

data source

All ball-by-ball data from Cricsheet, an open cricket data project. IPL matches only. Player full names resolved via Wikidata.
