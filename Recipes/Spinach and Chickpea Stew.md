# Spinach and Chickpea Stew

A warming one-pot stew of spinach and chickpeas braised in a ginger-tomato base with smoked paprika — perfect for cold days and crusty bread.

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 40-45 minutes
**Servings:** 6

## Ingredients

### The Base

- 6 tbsp (90 ml) extra virgin olive oil, plus more for serving
- 2 medium yellow or white onions, finely diced
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp (15 ml) sweet smoked paprika (pimentón de la Vera)
- Salt to taste
- Pinch of MSG (optional)

### The Vegetables & Legumes

- 1 (28 oz / 794g) can crushed tomatoes
- 1.5 cups (355 ml) vegetable stock (optional, for a brothier stew)
- 1.5 in (4 cm) fresh ginger, peeled and sliced into coins
- 11 oz (312g) fresh spinach (curly leaf preferred)
- 1 (19 oz / 540 ml) can chickpeas, undrained (or one 28 oz / 800 ml can for a heartier stew)
- 3 bay leaves

### The Finish

- 1 tbsp (15 ml) sherry vinegar (sub: cider or white wine vinegar)
- Crusty bread for serving

## Instructions

1. Pour about half the can of crushed tomatoes into a blender jar. Add the ginger. Blend smooth. Set the rest of the can aside.

2. Heat the olive oil in a Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic. Cook 5-7 minutes, stirring now and then, until soft but not browned. Add salt, MSG if using, and paprika. Stir and let the spices bloom for 30 seconds.

3. Pour in the tomato-ginger puree and stir well.

4. Add the spinach in batches, stirring each handful down before adding the next.

5. Once all the spinach has wilted, add the remaining crushed tomatoes, the chickpeas with all their liquid, and the bay leaves. Add the vegetable stock now if using. Bring to a boil, then drop to a simmer. Cook uncovered 40-45 minutes until thick and rich.

6. Taste and adjust salt. Stir in the sherry vinegar. Ladle into bowls, drizzle with your best olive oil, and serve with toasted bread.
