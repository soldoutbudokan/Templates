# Lentil Soup

A plain, good lentil soup built on a fresh gremolata that works twice: once as the flavor base, once as the garnish.

**Servings:** 5
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 25-45 minutes

## Ingredients

- 250g brown or red lentils
- 1 onion, finely diced
- 1 carrot, finely diced
- 1 celery stalk, finely diced
- 1 medium potato, peeled and diced
- 1 litre chicken broth (or vegetable stock/water)
- 1 lemon (zest optional, juice required)
- 3 garlic cloves, crushed or minced
- 1 bunch fresh parsley, chopped
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- Pinch of MSG (optional)
- 2 bay leaves (optional)
- Crusty bread, buttered, to serve

## Instructions

1. If using, zest the lemon into a small bowl. Crush the garlic and add it to the bowl. Pour in a good slug of olive oil and stir well to separate the garlic bits (this slows allicin formation and keeps the garlic smoother). Chop the parsley, add most of it to the bowl, and set the rest aside. Season with salt, pepper, and MSG. Stir into a rough paste.

2. Rinse the lentils in a sieve under cold water and pick out any stones. Dice the onion, carrot, and celery into roughly ¼-inch pieces. Peel the potato and cut into small, even chunks.

3. Heat a few tablespoons of olive oil in a large pot over moderately high heat. Add the onion, carrot, and celery. Cook for 4 to 5 minutes until soft and translucent, not browned. Stir in about half the gremolata and a handful of the reserved parsley.

4. Add the lentils to the pot. Stir for a minute or two so they pick up the oil and aromatics.

5. Pour in the broth. Add the potato and, if using, the bay leaves. Bring to a simmer, then lower the heat and cook until the lentils and potatoes are tender (25 to 45 minutes depending on the lentil).

6. Pull out the bay leaves if you used them. Run the immersion blender through the soup until completely smooth. Squeeze in the juice of half the lemon and stir in the remaining parsley. Taste and adjust salt and pepper.

7. Ladle into bowls. Top with a spoonful of the remaining gremolata, a drizzle of good olive oil, and a crack of black pepper. Serve with buttered crusty bread.

## Notes

Red lentils give a warm orange-gold color and fall apart completely, which is fine since you're blending smooth. Brown lentils taste good but blend to a muddy color. For the prettiest smooth soup, use red. For a vegan version, use vegetable stock or water.
