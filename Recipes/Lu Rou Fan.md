# Simplified Lu Rou Fan (Meal Prep Version)

Taiwanese braised pork belly over rice, built for meal prep. One soy sauce, dry sherry, ground spices instead of a spice bag, and roasted broccoli on the side.

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes
**Cook Time:** 3 hours (mostly hands-off braise)
**Servings:** 6

## Ingredients

### The Pork Braise

- 750g pork belly, cut into 1 cm dice
- 3 shallots, thinly sliced
- 3 garlic cloves, smashed
- 6 coins fresh ginger, sliced
- 2 bay leaves
- 1/2 tsp Chinese five-spice powder (optional)
- 1/3 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1/3 tsp white pepper
- 50 ml dry sherry
- 60 ml Japanese soy sauce
- 30g dark brown sugar
- 250 ml water
- 6 eggs, hard-boiled and peeled (optional)

### The Rice

- 2 cups jasmine or short-grain rice, uncooked

### The Broccoli

- 700g broccoli, cut into florets with a flat side
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1/2 tsp salt

### To Assemble

- 2 scallions, sliced

## Instructions

1. **Render the pork:** Put the pork belly in a cold Dutch oven and set it over medium heat. Let the fat render and the meat brown, stirring now and then, 6 to 8 minutes. You want a decent pool of fat in the bottom and some color on the pork.

2. **Soften the shallots:** Add the shallots to the rendered fat. Cook until soft and deep golden, 5 to 6 minutes. They'll break down into the sauce later, so don't worry about keeping them intact.

3. **Bloom the aromatics:** Add the garlic, ginger, and bay leaves. Stir for 30 seconds, then add the five-spice, cinnamon, and white pepper. Stir another 30 seconds — ground spices burn fast, so move to the next step as soon as you can smell them.

4. **Deglaze and build the braise:** Pour in the sherry and scrape the bottom of the pot clean. Add the soy sauce and brown sugar, stir to dissolve the sugar, then add the water — just enough to barely submerge the pork. Bring to a simmer.

5. **Braise:** Partially cover and cook until the pork is completely tender. Oven at 300°F/150°C runs about 3 hours and needs almost no attention. Stovetop on a bare simmer takes closer to 2 hours — check every 30 minutes and add a splash of water if it's reducing too fast.

6. **Add the eggs:** In the last half hour, slip the hard-boiled eggs into the braise so they take on color and salt. Turn them once so they stain evenly.

7. **Cook the rice:** Rinse and steam the rice. Spread it on a sheet pan to cool before it goes into containers — rice left to steam in a sealed tub goes gummy.

8. **Roast the broccoli:** Heat the oven to 425°F/220°C. Toss the broccoli with the oil and salt, spread on a sheet pan cut-side down, and give the pieces room. Roast 18 to 20 minutes, until the flat sides are browned and the stems still have some resistance. Pull it a touch early — reheating softens it further.

9. **Reduce and cool:** If the sauce is thin, pull the eggs out, uncover, and simmer hard until it's glossy and coats a spoon, 10 to 15 minutes. Fish out the bay leaves and ginger coins. Cool the broccoli uncovered before it goes in a container.

10. **Assemble:** Pork and sauce over rice, broccoli alongside, eggs halved, scallions on top. If you're eating it later, add the scallions after reheating.

## Notes

On the cinnamon: ground spice loses its edge faster than a stick does, so if you're making this ahead the cinnamon will be more of a background hum by day three. That's fine — arguably better than the sharper hit a fresh stick gives.

Without dark soy the sauce lands a lighter mahogany than the classic near-black. To darken it, melt the brown sugar in the rendered fat until amber before adding the liquids.

Taste before the final reduction. Concentrating the sauce concentrates the salt.

Portioning: rice on the bottom, pork and sauce in the middle, broccoli in its own corner or a separate container. Eggs keep better whole in the braising liquid than halved. The pork improves through about day four; the broccoli peaks on day two.

## Nutrition (per serving)

*Based on 6 servings, with most of the rendered pork fat skimmed off the braise.*

| Nutrient | No egg | + 1 egg | + 2 eggs |
