# Parmesan-Crusted Roasted Potatoes

Kenji López-Alt's method, scaled down. Alkaline water breaks down the potato exterior into a starchy slurry; the cheese and butter turn that into a crust.

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 45-50 minutes
**Servings:** 4 (as a side)

## Ingredients

### The Boil

- 2 lbs Russet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1½–2 inch chunks
- 1.5 tbsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp baking soda
- 2 tsp whole black peppercorns
- 4 cloves garlic, peeled and lightly crushed
- 3 dried bay leaves
- 3 sprigs fresh rosemary (or thyme)

### The Crust

- 2 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
- 1.5 oz Parmesan, finely grated (about 3/4 cup)
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely minced

## Instructions

1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into large chunks, 1½ to 2 inches. Big chunks give you a fluffy interior under a thick crust. Put them in a large pot and cover with cold water by an inch.

2. Add the salt and baking soda to the pot, then drop in the peppercorns, garlic, bay leaves, and rosemary sprigs. Skip the cheesecloth pouch — you'll fish everything out at the colander stage. Bay leaves and rosemary sprigs float and stay whole, so they're easy to grab. Peppercorns are the fussy ones; a few strays won't hurt anything.

3. Bring to a boil, then drop to a simmer. Cook about 10 minutes, until a paring knife slides into a chunk with no resistance. Heat the oven now: 400°F on convection, or 425°F without.

4. Drain through a colander. Pull out the bay leaves, rosemary sprigs, garlic, and whatever peppercorns you can see. Let the potatoes steam-dry in the colander for a minute.

5. Tip the hot potatoes into a large bowl. Drizzle over the melted butter and scatter the Parmesan on top. Shake the bowl hard several times and stir roughly with a spatula. You want the outer layer of each chunk to break down into a thick, mashed-potato paste that coats everything. This roughing-up step is what makes the crust.

6. Spread the potatoes on a parchment-lined sheet pan in a single layer, with as much space between chunks as you can manage. Crowding steams them. Roast undisturbed for 20 minutes.

7. Slide a thin metal spatula (a fish spatula is ideal) under each potato and pry it up slowly. The melted cheese sticks to the parchment, so work patiently or you'll leave the crust behind. Flip each chunk over.

8. Back in the oven for 15 to 20 minutes. Watch the color near the end — you want deep golden brown, but Parmesan turns bitter if it goes too dark.

9. Tip the potatoes into a clean bowl, toss with the minced fresh rosemary, and serve hot.

## Notes

Yukon Golds work too. Avoid waxy red potatoes — they won't crisp. Swap the butter for duck fat, beef tallow, or olive oil if you'd rather. The baking soda is the whole trick: it raises the pH, which speeds the breakdown of pectin on the potato surface and gives you that starchy slurry to crisp up. Don't skip it.
